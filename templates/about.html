Our History

The Virasat Story

Virasat bridges the gap between ancient royal kitchens and contemporary fine dining.

Established with the vision of preserving India's culinary heritage, our journey began with deep research into the recipes of the Mughal and Rajput courts. We've spent years reviving forgotten cooking techniques like dum pukht and traditional slow charcoal roasting.

However, we are not just a museum of old recipes. At Virasat, we present these rich, complex flavours using modern plating and contemporary gastronomic techniques. Our interior is designed to be an approachable yet luxurious sanctuary—a modern stage for an ancient feast.
